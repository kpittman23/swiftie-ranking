    Harry Styles aside, 1989, Taylor's most award winning and record breaking album, was born out of a gut wrenching loss. Her 4th studio album, Red, lost to Daft Punk's "Random Access Memories" for Album of the Year at the 2014 Grammy's. During the 2015 GrammyPro listening sessions, Taylor talks about the inspiration for 1989. She says after her loss at the Grammy's, she woke up at 4 in the morning and she just knew: "it's called 1989, its 80s synth pop, I'm calling it a pop record, I'm not listening to anyone at my record label, and I'm starting tomorrow."

    Much of 1989 is a response to the way Taylor was treated by the media during the Red era. She was criticized for brandishing the intimate details of her past relationships, for being vindictive and clingy, and dating too much. During her acceptance speech for Woman of the Decade at the Billboard awards in 2019, she said :

    "Now they're saying my album Red is filled with too many breakup songs? Okay, okay, I'll make one about moving to New York and deciding that really my life is more fun with just my friends. Oh, they're saying my music is changing too much for me to stay in country music? All right. Okay, here's an entire genre shift and a pop album called 1989."

    1989 collected 3 Grammy Awards in 2016, including Album of the Year, Best Pop Vocal Album, and Best Music Video (Bad Blood). It had 3 number ones and 5 top tens.

    Ultimately, 1989 was the album that transformed Taylor from a quirky country artist to a full blown international phenomenon.
