    Speak Now delves into some new musical styles, including pop-rock and whatever "arena-rock" is.

    Taylor has described Speak Now as a loose concept album about the things she never had a chance to say. The title track, "Speak Now" has the narrator interrupting a wedding, where "Back To December" delivers a heartfelt apology to an old lover. "Dear John" and "Better Than Revenge" are less apologetic, more scathing.

    Despite "Mean" winning a grammy for Best Country Song, Speak Now is Taylor's worst performing album in terms of awards and accolades. It was generally well received by the public and music experts, but it was also criticized for not being sonically cohesive.

    The chaos of the album extended to Taylor's second arena tour. The Speak Now World Tour was the most extra tour of Taylor's career, and included a full wedding set, Taylor throwing a chair off a platform, and the iconic "Haunted" bell.

    Despite it's criticisms, Speak Now was an important step in Taylor's career, especially in regards to her willingness to experiment with different sounds and step out of the country box.
