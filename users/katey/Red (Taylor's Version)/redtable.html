    • Taylor dipped her toes into pop music with the making of Red, the first step in turning herself into an international pop icon.

    She worked with Max Martin and Shellback on the main pop numbers on Red: "22", "I Knew You Were Trouble" and "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together". The album was still marketed as a country album, however, and was thus criticized for not being cohesive.

    Taylor released Red (Taylor's Version) in November 2021, the second out of six re-recordings. It was released with 10 vault tracks, including some songs fans already knew, like "Ronan", a song from a cancer benefit, "Babe", originally sung by Sugarland, and "Better Man", originally sung by Little Big Town. The vault also includes a 10 minute version of All Too Well, which fans have been begging for ever since Taylor let it slip that there was a 10 minute version she hadn't released back in 2012.

    Upon release, Red (Taylor's Version) had all 28 tracks chart on the Billboard Hot 100. "All Too Well (10 Minute Version)" hit #1 on the Hot 100 and became the longest #1 of all time, beating out "American Pie" by Don McLean.

    Red (Taylor's Version) was a major pop-culture moment. It's no longer cool to hate Taylor Swift, as seen by the amount of companies and influencers using Red TV content for marketing and branding. Red TV overcame the misogyny that ran rampant in 2012 during its original release: all comments about Taylor being a "boy crazy, vengeful ex" turned into comments about her "masterclass in pop songwriting and poetry." Oh how the turn tables.

    Red is clearly very close to Taylor's heart. Red reflects a difficult time emotionally for Taylor, even further revealed by the vault tracks on the re-release. During the Red era in 2012, Taylor would often get emotional while performing All Too Well, even crying on stage during the performance at the Grammys in 2014. Now, she often performs it with a smile, noting in an instagram post: "it always blows my mind that it is consistently one of the loudest songs the crowd sings when I play it".
