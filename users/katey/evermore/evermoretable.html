    • evermore is a hard album to write about, simply because Taylor acts like it does not exist.

    On December 11, 2020, only 5 months after the release of folklore Taylor surprise released evermore tweeting: "I'm elated to tell you that my 9th studio album, and folklore's sister record, will be out tonight at midnight eastern. It's called evermore." with a picture of the back of her head, and then literally never spoke of it again.

    evermore was literally nominated for Album of the Year at the Grammys, and Taylor didn't even show up.

    In many ways, evermore is just an extension of folklore, perhaps even more like the "vault tracks" of the new Taylor's Versions of Red and Fearless. Because of this "vault track" feeling, evermore is a little bit less cohesive than folklore. Songs like "long story short" and "closure" stick out like sore thumbs in a list of folksy tracks.

    And while most of the other tracks stick with the folksy theme, the stories aren't intertwined the way they were on folklore. There's no high school love triangle, though there are some similarities in the stories from "'tis the damn season" and "dorothea". There's also a bit more of a mystical, witchy theme amongst songs like "willow", "ivy", and even "right where you left me".

    evermore has far more influence from The National than it's sister, evident in songs like "'tis the Damn Season" and "coney Island" which Matt Beringer (of The National) even sings on. The composition of evermore feels more like it was made for a band rather than a solo performer, which likely comes from the fact that the first two songs written, "closure" and "dorothea" were written for Big Red Machine, a band made up of Aaron Dessner (The National) and Justin Vernon (Bon Iver) named after the 1970s Cincinnati Reds.

    Ultimately, evermore, the silent sister of beloved and award winning folklore probably deserves a little more hype than she gets. She's bold, she's playful, and even just a bit more free. And as many fans will tell you, even though evermore isn't as good of an album as folklore, she's got the better songs.
