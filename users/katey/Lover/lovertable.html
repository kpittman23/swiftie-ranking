    • Lover never got the full era it deserved, thanks to COVID-19. Lover was supposed to be a bright and airy awakening following the darker reputation, and it was, but only for a brief time before a virus shut the world down.

    Lover was the first album Taylor owned after leaving Big Machine Records and signing with Republic. She described it as "a love letter to love itself" and noted that it was the first time she got to write about "love that was very real."

    She was set to perform a world tour in summer of 2020, but obviously that didn't happen. Instead, Taylor released folklore and evermore, launching us all into a cottage-core era.

    Lover has a few quirks, including the most chaotic run in Taylor's discography: "London Boy", "Soon You'll Get Better", and "False God", AKA "Corny Things About London", "My Mom Has Cancer", and "SEX". Lover also includes one of the most hated songs amongst fans: "ME!".

    Despite being an actual banger, fans and critics alike hate "ME!". Sure, the addition (and ultimate removal) of "Hey kids, spelling is fun!" is hard to listen to, but "ME!" is actually the perfect choice for the lead single for Lover.

    The stark transition from reputation to Lover is important. Taylor tells us: reputation was a persona. Lover is "ME!".
